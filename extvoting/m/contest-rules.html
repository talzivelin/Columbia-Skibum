2013 Columbia Ski Bum Scholarship Video Contest

Official Rules

NO PURCHASE NECESSARY. A PURCHASE OR PAYMENT OF ANY KIND WILL NOT INCREASE YOUR CHANCES OF WINNING.

  1. Eligibility:  Ski Bum Scholarship Program (the “Contest”) is open only to legal residents of the fifty (50) United States and the District of Columbia and legal residents of Canada (excluding Quebec) who are at least nineteen (19) years old at the time of entry (“Contestant”). United States Contestants and their Guest will require a valid United States passport to be eligible to win the Grand Prize.  Employees of Columbia Sportswear Company, CMD (“Administrator”), and any of their parent and affiliate companies as well as the immediate family (spouse, parents, siblings and children) and household members of each such employee are not eligible.  The Contest is subject to all applicable federal, state, provincial, and local laws and regulations and is void in Quebec, outside the U.S. and Canada and where prohibited.

    See Sponsor’s Privacy Policy at
    U.S.: http://www.columbia.com/Privacy-Policy/Privacy_Policy,default,pg.html.
    Canada: http://www.columbiasportswear.ca/Privacy-Policy/Privacy_Policy,en_CA,pg.html

  2. Sponsor: Columbia Sportswear Company, 14375 N.W. Science Park Dr., Portland, OR 97229. 

  3. Agreement to Official Rules: Participation in this Contest constitutes Contestant’s full and unconditional agreement to these Official Rules and Sponsor’s decisions, which are final and binding in all matters related to this Contest.  Winning a prize is contingent upon fulfilling all requirements set forth herein.

  4. Timing: The Contest begins on October 21, 2013 at 12:00 a.m. Eastern Time ("ET"), and ends on December 2, 2013 at 11:59 p.m. ET (the "Contest Period").

    Sponsor's computer is the official time-keeping device for the Contest.

  5. How to Enter: During the Contest Period, visit the Columbia Sportswear Company Ski Bum Scholarship website www.skibumscholarship.com (the “Website”) and follow the links and instructions to complete and submit the application form, including your video file based on the specs detailed on the Website.  The video you create is where you express why you are deserving of the 2013 Ski Bum Scholarship (SBS), hereafter referred to as your submission (your "Submission").

    After you have submitted your Submission and subject to Sponsor approval, your Submission will be posted for the Public Voting Period consistent with the details set forth in Section 6 of these Official Rules.

    By uploading your Submission, you agree that your Submission conforms to the Video Guidelines and Content Restrictions as defined below (collectively, the “Guidelines and Restrictions”) and that Sponsor, in its sole discretion, may remove your Submission and disqualify you from the Promotion if it believes that your Submission fails to conform to the Guidelines and Restrictions.

    Video Guidelines: 

    • The Submission must be in an accepted video file format as outlined on the Website;
    • The Submission must be at least one (1) minute and not exceed ninety (90) seconds in length; any music that is included on the video can be one of the five licensed tracks supplied in the “Swag Bag” zip folder and available for download on the Website.  The licensed tracks may not be altered or edited in any way. If any Video Submissions use music other than the tracks provided on the Website, the user must have express permission and license from the owner of the music track to use a particular music track(s). If Sponsor suspects that user has submitted a video with unlicensed music, the Video Submission is subject to disqualification.
    • The Submission cannot have been submitted previously in a promotion of any kind or exhibited or displayed publicly through any means.

    Content Restrictions:

    • The Submission must not contain material that violates or infringes another’s rights, including but not limited to privacy, publicity or intellectual property rights (such as music), or that constitutes copyright infringement;
    • The Submission must not disparage Sponsor or any other person or party affiliated with the Promotion;
    • The Submission must not contain content that conflicts with Sponsor’s corporate responsibility, mission, and/or values, all of which are detailed on Sponsor’s website located at www.columbia.com;
    • The Submission must not contain brand names or trademarks other than those owned by Sponsor, which Contestant has a limited license to use to incorporate in his/her Submission in this Contest;
    • The Submission must not contain images or artwork not created by Contestant;
    • The Submission must not contain material that is inappropriate, indecent, obscene, hateful, tortious, defamatory, slanderous or libelous;
    • The Submission must not contain material that promotes bigotry, racism, hatred or harm against any group or individual or promotes discrimination based on race, gender, religion, nationality, disability, sexual orientation or age; and
    • The Submission must not contain material that is unlawful, in violation of or contrary to the laws or regulations in any state or province where the Submission is created.
    • The Submission may not include, without properly executed releases, parties who: 1) are represented under contract (e.g., by a talent agent or manager) that would limit or impair our ability to show the Submission in any media form; 2) have an acting or modeling contract that would make their appearance in the Submission, Contest, or any related uses by Sponsor a violation of any third party rights; 3) are under a merchandising agreement; or 4) are under any other contractual relationship, including but not limited to guild and/or union memberships, that may prohibit them from participating fully in this promotion, or from allowing Sponsor to use the Submission online, in television media, and other forms of distribution at Sponsor’s discretion.

    Limit: Each Contestant may upload one (1) Submission during the Contest Period.  Submissions received from any person or email address in excess of the stated limit will be void. Submissions generated by script, macro or other automated means are void. Contestant must have permission from all individuals that appear in the Submission (if any) to use their name and likeness in the Submission and to grant the rights set forth herein.  If requested, Contestant must be able to provide such permissions in a form acceptable to Sponsor.

Each Contestant may be required to show proof of being an authorized account holder.

  6. Public Voting:  After you have submitted your Submission and subject to Sponsor approval, your Submission will be posted in a gallery on the Website where the public will be invited to vote on which Submission they believe has the most public appeal (the “Public Voting Period”).  The Public Voting Period will extend to December 8, 2013.  The number of votes your Submission receives will be counted towards your Submission's score in the Public Voting category of the Judging Criteria, as outlined in Section 7 below, but will not be determinative of which entries are ultimately chosen.  Each member of the public will receive one vote per natural person per day for the duration of the Public Voting Period.

    If Sponsor suspects or has knowledge that you purchased, bartered for, or otherwise offered consideration or used an online voting service or group to obtain votes for your Submission, your Submission is subject to disqualification in Sponsor’s sole discretion.

  7. Winner Determination: Following the Public Voting Period as outlined in Section 6 of these Official Rules, a panel of qualified judges selected by Sponsor in its sole discretion will select eleven (11) Contestants (“Selected Contestants”) with the highest-scoring Submissions from among all eligible Submissions based on the following criteria (“Judging Criteria”):

    • Creativity/Originality (30%);
    • Consistency with Sponsor’s Corporate/Social Responsibility & Values (20%);
    • Fit to Contest Theme (20%); and
    • Public Voting (30%)

    The Contestant with the highest-scoring Submission will be deemed the potential Grand Prize Selected Contestant, and the ten (10) Contestants with the next highest-scoring Submissions will be deemed potential Finalists. In the event of a tie, the Contestant whose Submission received the highest score for Consistency with Sponsor’s Corporate/Social Responsibility & Values, as determined by the qualified judges, in their sole discretion, will be deemed the applicable potential Selected Contestant. Sponsor reserves the right to select fewer than eleven (11) potential Selected Contestants, if, in its sole discretion, it does not receive a sufficient number of eligible and qualified Submissions.
